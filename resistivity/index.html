IBC Chapter 18 requires a rational foundation design based on adequate subsurface data — and in the Paso del Norte region, assumptions about uniform soil can be expensive. The Rio Grande alluvium, with its lenses of coarse gravel, silty clay, and caliche, doesn't behave predictably. That's why our electrical resistivity surveys in El Paso start from the premise that what you can't see will cost you. The VES method measures the apparent resistivity of subsurface layers by injecting current and reading potential difference, producing a 1D profile that identifies transitions between materials of contrasting resistivity. In the Franklin Mountains foothills, for example, a MASW survey might pair with resistivity data to cross-check bedrock depth, while a CPT test can calibrate resistivity values against tip resistance in the soft basin fill. The ASTM D6431 standard guides field procedures and data inversion, and we apply it rigorously — because when the monsoon season hits the arroyos, you need to know exactly where the water table sits beneath your pad.

In the basin-fill environment of El Paso, a VES survey often finds the water table within 30 ft — miss it, and your excavation plan is already underwater.

Method and coverage

Comparing two areas of El Paso illustrates why resistivity mapping matters. On the Westside, near the Mesilla Valley shear zone, the subsurface transitions from desert pavement gravels to deep, conductive silts within 15 to 30 feet. A VES sounding here often shows a sharp resistivity drop at the water table — typically between 10 and 25 ohm-m — which tells the engineer exactly where to anticipate buoyant forces on a mat foundation. Over on the Eastside, the Hueco Bolson deposits are thicker and more uniform, but caliche layers create high-resistivity caps that can deflect current and mask deeper anomalies. We adjust the Schlumberger array spacing to punch through those resistive lenses and reach the saturated zone. This is where a grain-size analysis of cuttings from a nearby boring becomes invaluable: it lets us correlate the resistivity signature with actual particle size distribution, so the geotechnical model isn't just an inversion — it's ground-truthed data an engineer can stake a design on.

Regional considerations

The Hueco Bolson aquifer has supplied El Paso for decades, but it also means groundwater is a constant variable in construction. In the downtown area, depth to water can be as shallow as 15 to 25 feet — and seasonal fluctuations tied to the Rio Grande irrigation cycle can shift that level by several feet within a month. A resistivity survey that doesn't account for these seasonal variations can misplace the water table by a full story of excavation. That's a problem when you're designing a two-level basement next to the I-10 corridor. We've seen projects where a missed low-resistivity lens — a pocket of saturated silt within a gravel formation — led to unexpected dewatering costs that ate 12% of the earthwork budget. The VES method, when run with a tight AB/2 spacing sequence and correlated with in-situ permeability tests, gives you the vertical resolution to spot those lenses before the backhoe does. In El Paso's desert environment, where flash floods can saturate arroyo sediments in hours, that kind of advance intel isn't optional — it's the difference between a dry excavation and an unplanned sump pump operation.

Electrical Resistivity Tomography (ERT) — 2D Cross-Section Imaging

Multi-electrode Wenner or dipole-dipole array along a survey line, producing a continuous resistivity cross-section. Used for pipeline alignments, levee investigations, and fault trace mapping in the East Franklin Mountain piedmont zone. Output includes a 2D inverted resistivity section with topographic correction and RMS error analysis.

Quick answers

How deep can a VES survey investigate in El Paso's basin-fill soils?

Practical investigation depth depends on the current injection capability and the available space to extend current electrodes. In El Paso's Hueco Bolson deposits, where surface gravels are moderately resistive (typically 50-200 ohm-m), we routinely reach 150 to 300 feet with an AB/2 spacing of up to 600 feet. Deeper targets are possible, but cultural noise from power lines and pipelines along the I-10 corridor can reduce data quality at large spacings. We'll assess site conditions during the pre-survey walk to confirm achievable depth.

Can electrical resistivity distinguish between caliche, gravel, and clay in El Paso?

Yes — and that's one of the main reasons engineers request this survey in the region. Caliche (calcium carbonate-cemented soil) typically registers above 100 ohm-m and can exceed 500 ohm-m when dry. Clean gravels in the Rio Grande alluvium fall in the 50-200 ohm-m range. Saturated silts and clays drop below 30 ohm-m, often into single digits. The contrast is distinct enough that a properly inverted VES curve can separate these units, especially when calibrated against a boring log or a grain-size analysis from the same site.

What's the typical turnaround time for a resistivity survey report?

For a standard VES sounding (single point), we deliver the preliminary resistivity-depth curve within 3 to 4 business days after field acquisition. A final interpreted report with stratigraphic recommendations follows within 7 business days. ERT lines covering 500 to 1,000 linear feet take 5 to 7 business days for preliminary data and up to 10 business days for the final report. Rush turnaround is available — just let us know your submittal deadline when you schedule.

Do you need access to both sides of the property for the VES survey?

The Schlumberger array requires laying out current electrodes symmetrically from a central point, so we do need clear access in two opposite directions. For a 300-foot investigation depth, plan for roughly 600 feet of clear line — 300 feet on each side of the sounding center. If site constraints limit access (tight urban lots in central El Paso, for example), we can adapt with an asymmetrical array or switch to a Wenner configuration, though this may reduce maximum penetration depth. We'll evaluate the site during the initial walk to determine the best approach.

What does a VES survey cost for a typical El Paso construction project?

For a single VES sounding in the El Paso area, budget between US$630 and US$930 depending on access conditions, target depth, and whether we're correlating with existing boring data. ERT lines are priced per linear foot and typically fall in a comparable range when normalized to investigation scale. Every site gets a fixed-price proposal after we review the location and project requirements — no hourly billing surprises.
